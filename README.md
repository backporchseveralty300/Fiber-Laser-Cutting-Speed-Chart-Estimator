## 💡 Best practices for laser cutting

Use the estimated feed rates to improve your margins. Faster speeds reduce heat buildup in thin materials. Slower speeds often improve edge quality on thick plates. Monitor your nozzle condition during testing. A worn nozzle affects the validity of these estimates. Regularly clean your protective window to ensure consistent power output at the material surface. Combine this tool with your daily machine inspection to maintain consistent parts production. 

This estimator simplifies the process of finding starting parameters. Spend less time in manual tables and more time producing parts. Ensure your gas flow matches the recommended pressures for the estimated speed. If the cut carries too much slag, slightly increase the feed rate. If the laser loses the cut, decrease the speed. Observe the spark pattern to determine if the jet provides enough energy for the selected thickness. Keep organized notes on your results to create a library of successful settings for your specific laser model. This habit helps you predict future project needs with high accuracy. The goal of this tool is to provide a reliable starting point for your daily machining tasks.
